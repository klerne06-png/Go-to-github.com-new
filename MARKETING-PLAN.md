## 1. Social Media Growth Strategy

### Best Platforms (ranked by ROI)
- **TikTok** — Highest-growth platform for food/beverage businesses. Drink-making process videos routinely get 100K+ views even from small accounts. The "sisters who started a business at 12 & 14" origin story is exactly the type of content TikTok amplifies.
- **Instagram** — Essential for bookings. Reels get 2-3x the reach of static posts. Event planners, brides, and corporate coordinators search and vet vendors here.
- **Facebook** — Critical for the 35-55 demographic that books corporate events and weddings. Facebook Events and local community groups drive direct bookings.
- **YouTube Shorts** — Repurpose TikTok/Reels content here for additional reach at zero extra effort.

### Content Types That Perform Best
- **Process videos** — Pouring, shaking, layering drinks. ASMR-adjacent "satisfying pour" content consistently goes viral.
- **Behind-the-scenes setup/teardown** — "Setting up our trailer at 5am for a 10,000 person festival"
- **Origin story content** — "We started this business at 12 & 14 years old" — create 15-sec, 60-sec, and 3-min versions. Repost quarterly.
- **Customer reaction videos** — Film people's first sip. Get permission and tag them.
- **Event recap montages** — 15-30 second highlight reels from each event. Tag the event, venue, and organizer.
- **Menu reveals and seasonal launches** — "NEW drink drop" content with visual appeal.
- **Day-in-the-life** — "A day in the life of teenage business owners" is a proven TikTok format.

### Posting Frequency
- TikTok: 4-7 times per week
- Instagram: 3-5 Reels/week, 2-3 Stories/day on event days, 1-2 static posts/week
- Facebook: 3-4 posts per week, share to relevant local groups

### Hashtag Strategy
- 5-10 hashtags per post on Instagram
- Mix of: branded (#WhatsShaking #WhatsBrewin), local (#YourCityEvents), niche (#MobileBeverage #LemonShakeUp #FoodTruckLife), and trending tags
- Create a customer hashtag like #ShakeItWithUs
- TikTok: 3-5 hashtags max, lean into trending sounds

### Quick Wins
- Film 20-30 short clips at every event (pouring shots, crowd shots, setup). Edit into multiple pieces later.
- Respond to every comment in the first hour after posting.
- Weekly "Where We'll Be" story/post.
- Collaborate with local food bloggers — free drinks in exchange for content.

## 2. Booking Corporate Events & Weddings

### Marketing Channels That Drive Bookings
- **The Knot & WeddingWire** — #1 and #2 places brides search. Paid listings ($50-150/month). Many mobile bar businesses report 30-50% of wedding bookings from these platforms.
- **Instagram DMs** — The #2 source of wedding bookings. Brides search hashtags like #CityWeddingCaterer, #MobileBarCity.
- **Google Business Profile** — Rank for "mobile beverage catering near me" and "lemonade cart wedding."
- **Direct outreach to event planners** — Send physical mailers with pro photos and a one-sheet.
- **Venue partnerships** — Get on "preferred vendor" lists at wedding venues and conference centers. This is the single highest-converting channel.
- **Facebook community groups** — Join every local "wedding planning" and "party planning" group.
- **Catering marketplaces** — Roaming Hunger, Best Food Trucks, etc.

### What Makes Clients Choose You
- Professional photos (invest $300-500 in a photoshoot of both trailers)
- Clear, professional PDF/webpage with packages and pricing tiers
- Testimonials and reviews from every event
- Quick response time — respond within 2 hours (first vendor to respond books the job 35-50% of the time)
- Your story — "Two sisters who started as teenagers" is a huge differentiator
- Customization — custom drink menus, branded cups, signage matching event themes

### How to Pitch Corporate Clients
- Target companies with 50+ employees — HR departments and office managers book events
- Offer a free/discounted "sample event" — show up at their office for 2 hours
- Target: employee appreciation days, company picnics, holiday parties, product launches
- Send a one-page proposal: 3 package options, photos, story, 2-3 testimonials
- Follow up at day 3, then day 7

## 3. SEO & Google Business Profile

### Google Business Profile (Most Important Free Marketing Tool)
- Create TWO separate profiles — one for each brand
- Primary category: "Mobile Caterer" or "Beverage Supplier"
- Upload 20+ high-quality photos, update monthly
- Post weekly: upcoming events, new menu items, seasonal specials
- Pre-populate Q&A with common questions
- Goal: 50+ reviews on each profile

### Website SEO Keywords to Target
- "mobile beverage catering [your city]"
- "lemon shake up catering"
- "mobile coffee cart wedding [your city]"
- "food truck catering corporate events [your city]"
- "wedding drink cart [your city]"
- "lemonade stand catering [your state]"

### Content Strategy
- Create dedicated "Wedding Packages" and "Corporate Events" pages
- Blog 1-2 posts/month: "5 Unique Wedding Drink Ideas," "Why a Mobile Coffee Cart is Perfect for Corporate Events"
- Add schema markup for "FoodEstablishment" and "CateringService"

## 4. Email Marketing & CRM

### Building Your Email List
- QR code at every event: "Join our VIP list — get notified where we'll be next"
- Incentive: "$1 off your next drink" or "Enter to win free drinks for a year"
- Collect emails from every inquiry, even if they don't book

### Email Sequences
- **Welcome (automated, 3 emails):** Origin story > Menu highlights > "Did you know we cater weddings?"
- **Weekly "Where We'll Be"** — Your highest open-rate email
- **Post-event follow-up:** Thank you + photos > Review request > Rebooking discount

### Recommended Tools
- Mailchimp (free up to 500 contacts) or Flodesk ($38/month)
- HoneyBook or Dubsado ($20-40/month) for proposals, contracts, and invoicing

## 6. Pricing Strategies

### Wedding Packages
- **Bronze:** 2 hours, up to 75 guests, standard menu — $800-1,200
- **Silver:** 3 hours, up to 150 guests, custom menu + branded cups — $1,500-2,500
- **Gold:** 4 hours, up to 250 guests, full custom menu + cups + signage — $2,500-4,000
- **Add-ons:** Custom cup printing (+$1-2/cup), custom signage (+$75-150), extra hour (+$200-400)

### Corporate Packages
- Per-person: $6-12 per person for 1-2 hour service
- Flat rate: $500-1,500 for 2-3 hours
- Recurring discount: 10-15% off for monthly/quarterly bookings
- "Office Coffee Bar" morning service: $400-800 for 2-hour window

### Pricing Tips
- Always present 3 options (most people pick the middle)
- On website say "Packages starting at $X" and "Request a custom quote"
- Don't publish exact prices — lets you adjust based on the event

## 7. What Successful Mobile Beverage Businesses Do Differently

1. **Visually distinctive trailer** — It's an Instagram backdrop and a billboard
2. **Systematize everything** — Online booking, automated follow-ups, template proposals
3. **Create recurring revenue** — Monthly corporate coffee service, standing venue arrangements
4. **Build a media-worthy story** — Pitch to local news ("teenage sisters build two businesses")
5. **Diversify revenue** — Merch, packaged products (bottled lemonade, cold brew), catering add-ons
6. **Say no to low-margin events** — Track revenue-per-hour, stop attending unprofitable events
7. **Collect and use data** — Track what sells, when, at which events

## 8. Paid Advertising

### Facebook & Instagram Ads
- **Wedding Campaign:** Target engaged women 22-35, your service radius + 50 miles. Budget: $5-15/day. One $1,500 wedding booking pays for months of ads.
- **Corporate Campaign:** Target job titles "Office Manager," "HR," "Event Coordinator." Budget: $5-10/day.
- **Brand Awareness:** Target local audience interested in food festivals. Budget: $3-5/day.
- **Retargeting:** Install Facebook Pixel on website. Show ads to visitors who didn't inquire. Budget: $2-3/day. Highest ROI ad spend.

### Google Ads
- Search ads for "mobile beverage catering [city]," "wedding drink cart [city]"
- Budget: $10-20/day
- Send to dedicated landing pages, not homepage

### Total Budget: Start at $300-500/month, track every booking source, double down on what works.

## 9. Review & Reputation Management

### Getting Reviews
- Goal: 50+ reviews on each Google Business Profile within 12 months
- Ask after every event via personal text/email with direct link
- QR code on trailer linking to Google review page
- NFC tap card ($5-15) — customers tap phone to open review page
- Automated email 3-5 days after catered events

### Review Platforms (priority order)
1. Google Business Profile
2. Facebook Recommendations
3. The Knot / WeddingWire (for wedding clients)
4. Yelp (claim profile, don't actively solicit)

### Amplification
- Screenshot great reviews, share as Instagram Stories
- Create a "Testimonials" highlight on Instagram
- Add best quotes to website and proposals
- Respond to EVERY review within 48 hours

## 10. Seasonal Strategy — Year-Round Revenue

### Your Biggest Advantage: Two Brands That Cover the Full Calendar
- **What's Shakin'** (cold drinks) = Peak April-October
- **What's Brewin'** (coffee/hot drinks) = Peak October-April

### Spring/Summer (March-September)
- Festival season, wedding season (May-Oct), corporate outdoor picnics
- Community events: 4th of July, county fairs, sporting events
- Add iced coffee/cold brew from What's Brewin' to summer events

### Fall (September-November)
- Pumpkin spice, apple cider, fall-themed menus
- Fall festivals, football tailgating
- Push holiday party bookings NOW (book Nov-Jan events in advance)

### Winter (December-February)
- Holiday parties (corporate + private) — hot chocolate bar, peppermint lattes
- Winter wedding packages
- Indoor events: conferences, trade shows, office buildings
- **Monthly Office Coffee subscription**: $400-800/month for weekly morning service

### Year-Round Stabilizers
- Recurring corporate accounts (monthly/quarterly service)
- Farmer's markets (indoor in winter)
- Packaged products (bottled lemonade concentrate, cold brew, drink mixes)
- Merch (shirts, stickers, cups)
- Catering delivery (pre-made drink carafes for smaller events)
